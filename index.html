Social Impacts of Artificial Organs

What are Artificial Organs?

Artificial organs are defined as an engineered device that can be implanted or integrated into a human body, used to replace a natural organ and duplicate or augment a specific function so the patient may return to a normal life as soon as possible.

There is a large range of organs that have been created, including hearts, kidneys and even external organs such as ears and eyes. They can be divided into three main classes: mechanical, bioartificial, or biomechanical. This is based on what types of materials are used to create the organ, which can be either artificial polymers or organic cells.

We had chosen this topic because we had found it interesting that the advancement of technology has allowed for the development of better artificial organs, but there are also several risks and potential negative implications on patients.
